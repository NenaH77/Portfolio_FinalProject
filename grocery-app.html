Grocery App UX Case Study

Improving the in-store grocery shopping experience through user-centered design.

Project Type

UX Coursework

My Role

Lead UX Designer

Focus Areas

Research, Wireframes, Prototyping, UI Design

Overview

This project focused on improving the in-store grocery shopping experience by designing a user-friendly app and responsive website that provides real-time inventory updates, interactive store maps, and personalized recommendations.

The Problem

In-store shopping can be inefficient due to crowded environments, changing layouts, and limited access to real-time product availability. Users often struggle to quickly find items, navigate the store, and complete their shopping efficiently.

The Goal

The goal was to streamline the shopping experience by creating a digital solution that improves navigation, saves time, and creates a more enjoyable experience for shoppers.

My Process

01

Research

I reviewed user needs, pain points, and shopping behaviors to better understand where the experience was breaking down.

02

Wireframes

I explored layout ideas, user flows, and screen structure before moving into polished interface designs.

03

Testing & Refinement

I used usability insights to improve navigation, clarity, accessibility, and the overall shopping flow.

Key Features

Real-time product availability Interactive store maps Personalized recommendations Simplified navigation

Design Takeaway

This project helped me better understand how small design decisions can improve usability, accessibility, and overall user confidence. Through research, testing, and iteration, I focused on creating an experience that felt clear, practical, and easy to use.
